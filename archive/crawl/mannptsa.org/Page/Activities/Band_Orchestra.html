Band and Orchestra Information Night

Date: September 24, 6:30-7:30 pm
Location: Horace Mann Gym

At the information night, you will learn about the band and orchestra programs at Horace Mann.  Your student will have the opportunity to try out different instruments and be measured for their instrument.  If you already know the instrument your child will be playing, there will be an opportunity to arrange a rental agreement right at the meeting. (instrument pick up at Music and Arts)

Instrument Rentals & Materials

Families are responsible for providing their own instrument, stand, and book for Band and Orchestra.  Music and Arts in Redmond is one option that will offer affordable instrument rentals for our students.  Representatives from Music and Arts will be at the information meeting or you can drop by their store any time to get your materials. 16611 Redmond Way Redmond, WA 98052 (425) 881-8777 Note: If you order online make sure and indicate your are from Horace Mann Elementary School to get the special rental deal ($5 first month, then $28 monthly)

If monthly rental is difficult financially for your family, the PTSA has offered to help find instruments to lend or give your student.  Please, reach out to enrichment@mannptsa.org if you would like assistance finding an instrument for your student.  Please include the type and size of instrument you are looking for in your request e-mail.

Band at Horace Mann

Enrollment: Sept 7-26
Classes:  Oct 6 - June 4
Performances: February 19, 7-8pm & June 4 6-7pm in the Horace Mann Cafeteria

Band will offer 4 instrument options for students to learn: trumpet, trombone, clarinet and flute.  Although, the band will eventually practice and perform together, they will begin separated by Brass instruments on Tuesdays and woodwind instruments on Thursdays afterschool.  There will be 2 performances during the year.  Dates TBD. 

Orchestra at Horace Mann

Enrollment: Sept 7-26
Classes:  Oct 6 - March 25
Performances: TBD

Two orchestra ensembles are available for students: one beginner and one intermediate. Each will meet once a week and be offered to grades 2-5. Second grade is an excellent time to introduce kids to an instrument.  This will allow students to have multiple years at the elementary school building skills and making great memories playing music and can confidently go on to enjoy playing in their middle school and high school ensembles.

The beginning ensemble will teach instrument fundamentals including proper posture and technique and would be geared towards kids with no prior experience. They will then learn a few short pieces to play together as a group.

The intermediate ensemble will build upon and strengthen previously learned skills. This will allow kids to stay inspired and engaged as they explore playing more complex music. Within the intermediate group we can differentiate by assigning more or less difficult parts to students.

Q&A

What if my child changes his mind and wants to do a different instrument after class has already started?

After the first week, it is not advisable for a student to change instruments because the lessons will have already moved passed the basics and it may be hard for your child to catch up.  This will be handled on a case-by-case basis so please e-mail your instructor if your child is wanting to switch.
